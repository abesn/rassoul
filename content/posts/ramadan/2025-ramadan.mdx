## The Significance of Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, and it holds a unique status because the Quran was revealed during this month. Allah says:

<Arabic>{"شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْءَانُ هُدًى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَـٰتٍ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ"}</Arabic>

> *Shahru ramadāna lladhī unzila fīhi l-qur’ānu hudal lin-nāsi wa bayyinātin mina l-hudā wa l-furqān*  
> The month of <em>Ramadan</em> in which was revealed the Qur’ân, a guidance for mankind and clear proofs for the guidance and the criterion (between right and wrong). <Citation source="Quran 2:185" book="Quran" number="2:185" href="https://quran.com/2/185" />

This verse establishes the foundational role of Ramadan as a time of divine guidance. The revelation of the Quran began in this month, specifically on Laylat al-Qadr, the Night of Decree, which Allah describes as a blessed night:

<Arabic>{"إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ فِى لَيْلَةٍ مُّبَـٰرَكَةٍ ۚ إِنَّا كُنَّا مُنذِرِينَ"}</Arabic>

> *Innā anzalnāhu fī laylatin mubārakatin innā kunnā mundhirīn*  
> We sent it (this Qur’ân) down on a blessed night... <Citation source="Quran 44:3" book="Quran" number="44:3" href="https://quran.com/44/3" />

This blessed night, which occurs during the last ten nights of Ramadan, is when the Quran was first sent down from the Preserved Tablet to the lowest heaven, and from there revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ over approximately 23 years.

## Determining the Start of Ramadan 2025

The beginning of Ramadan is established by the sighting of the new moon, as indicated by the practice of the Prophet ﷺ. While the specific hadith from our verified sources does not contain complete text, the general method relies on lunar sighting. Muslims are encouraged to seek the moon on the 29th day of Sha‘ban (the month preceding Ramadan) and, if sighted, begin fasting the following day. If not sighted, they complete 30 days of Sha‘ban.

## Obligations and Blessings of Fasting

Fasting during Ramadan is an obligatory act of worship for every adult Muslim who is physically able. As stated in the same verse:

<Arabic>{"فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ"}</Arabic>

> *Faman shahida minkumu sh-shahra falyasumhu*  
> So whoever of you sights (the crescent on the first night of) the month (of <em>Ramadan</em> i.e. is present at his home), he must observe Saum (fasts) that month. <Citation source="Quran 2:185" book="Quran" number="2:185" href="https://quran.com/2/185" />

The fast is from dawn (Fajr) until sunset (Maghrib), during which Muslims abstain from food, drink, and sexual relations. The verse also provides concessions for those who are ill or traveling: they may break their fast and make up the missed days later. Allah’s intention is ease, not hardship:

<Arabic>{"يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ بِكُمُ ٱلْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ ٱلْعُسْرَ"}</Arabic>

> *Yurīdu llāhu bikumul-yusra wa lā yurīdu bikumul-‘usra*  
> Allâh intends for you ease, and He does not want to make things difficult for you. <Citation source="Quran 2:185" book="Quran" number="2:185" href="https://quran.com/2/185" />

Fasting is not merely physical abstinence; it is a spiritual discipline that cultivates taqwa (God-consciousness), self-restraint, and empathy for the less fortunate. The month offers multiplied rewards for every good deed, making it an ideal time for increased charity, prayer, Quran recitation, and seeking forgiveness.

## Spiritual Opportunities in Ramadan 2025

The last ten nights of Ramadan hold special significance, particularly Laylat al-Qadr, which is better than a thousand months. Worshipping on this night brings immense blessings, and Muslims are encouraged to increase their efforts in prayer, supplication, and Quran recitation during these nights. The exact date is unknown but is traditionally sought in the odd-numbered nights of the last ten, especially the 27th night.

Additionally, Ramadan is a time for community and family. The pre-dawn meal (suhoor) and the meal to break the fast (iftar) are communal acts that strengthen bonds. Giving charity (zakat and sadaqah) during this month is highly recommended, as rewards are multiplied.

## Practical Preparation for Ramadan 2025

To make the most of Ramadan 2025, Muslims can begin preparing beforehand by:

1. Increasing voluntary fasts in Sha‘ban to train the body and soul.
2. Reciting Quran regularly to build momentum for increased recitation during Ramadan.
3. Making sincere repentance and setting spiritual goals, such as completing the Quran, offering nightly prayers (taraweeh), and increasing charitable giving.
4. Arranging schedules to accommodate worship, including adjusting work or school commitments for early mornings and late nights.
5. Learning about the rulings of fasting to ensure worship is performed correctly.

The month culminates with Eid al-Fitr, a day of celebration, gratitude, and charity. Before Eid, Muslims are required to pay Zakat al-Fitr, a specific charity given to the poor so that all can share in the joy of the day.
