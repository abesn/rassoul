Ramadan 2026 is expected to begin on the evening of Tuesday, 17 February 2026, with the first day of fasting on Wednesday, 18 February 2026, subject to the sighting of the crescent moon in your region. The month will conclude with Eid al-Fitr around 19 or 20 March 2026, again depending on local moon sighting.

## When Does Ramadan 2026 Begin

The Islamic calendar is lunar, so Ramadan moves earlier by roughly ten or eleven days each Gregorian year. For 1447 AH, astronomical calculations place the new crescent moon of Ramadan on 17 February 2026. Most communities will perform Taraweeh prayers that night and begin their fast at dawn on 18 February.

However, the start of Ramadan is not finalised by calculation alone. Many mosques and national councils still wait for a confirmed local sighting of the hilal, so the date may shift by a day in some regions. It is wise to follow the announcement of a trustworthy local authority rather than relying solely on printed calendars.

Allah ties the obligation of fasting directly to the appearance of the month:

*Shahru Ramadana alladhi unzila fihi al-Qur'an, hudan lin-nasi wa bayyinatin mina al-huda wal-furqan. Faman shahida minkumu ash-shahra falyasumhu.*

"The month of Ramadan in which was revealed the Qur'an, a guidance for mankind and clear proofs for the guidance and the criterion. So whoever of you sights the month, he must observe fasts that month." <Citation source="Quran" book="Al-Baqarah" number="2:185" href="https://quran.com/2/185" />

## Why Ramadan Holds Such Weight

Ramadan is not simply a month of hunger and thirst. It is the month in which the Qur'an was first sent down, the month containing Laylat al-Qadr, and the only month named explicitly in the Qur'an in the context of an obligatory act of worship.

Allah describes the night of revelation:

<Arabic>{"إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ فِى لَيْلَةٍ مُّبَـٰرَكَةٍ ۚ إِنَّا كُنَّا مُنذِرِينَ"}</Arabic>

*Inna anzalnahu fi laylatin mubarakatin, inna kunna mundhirin.*

"We sent it down on a blessed night. Verily, We are ever warning." <Citation source="Quran" book="Ad-Dukhan" number="44:3" href="https://quran.com/44/3" />

This blessed night sits inside Ramadan, which is why the Prophet ﷺ and the early generations gave the last ten nights of the month a status unlike any other portion of the year. To witness Ramadan is, in a real sense, to stand at the doorway of the night on which the Qur'an was sent down.

## Key Dates to Plan Around in Ramadan 2026

While exact dates depend on the moon, here is a working timeline for 1447 AH:

- **First Taraweeh:** evening of Tuesday, 17 February 2026
- **First fast (1 Ramadan):** Wednesday, 18 February 2026
- **Start of the last ten nights (21 Ramadan):** evening of Saturday, 7 March 2026
- **Most likely odd nights for Laylat al-Qadr:** 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, and 29th of Ramadan
- **Expected Eid al-Fitr:** Thursday, 19 March 2026 or Friday, 20 March 2026

Treat these dates as a planning aid. Confirm with your local masjid once the Sha'ban moon is sighted in late January 2026, which sets the countdown to Ramadan itself.

## Preparing Spiritually Before Ramadan Arrives

The companions reportedly prepared for Ramadan for six months before it arrived, and then spent six months after it asking Allah to accept what they had offered. Preparation in the weeks leading up to 1 Ramadan 1447 can include:

- **Voluntary fasts in Sha'ban.** Train your body and routine so that the first week of Ramadan is not a shock.
- **Rebuilding your relationship with the Qur'an.** Aim for a fixed daily portion now so that a full khatm during Ramadan feels natural rather than forced.
- **Settling debts and disputes.** Enter the month with a clean slate between you and others, not only between you and Allah.
- **Reviewing the fiqh of fasting.** Refresh your knowledge on what breaks the fast, who is exempt, and how to make up missed days.

Allah Himself frames the rulings of fasting with mercy in the same verse that obligates it: *"Allah intends for you ease, and He does not want to make things difficult for you."* <Citation source="Quran" book="Al-Baqarah" number="2:185" href="https://quran.com/2/185" />

This is worth holding onto. Ramadan is demanding, but the legislation around it is built on ease for the traveller, the sick, the pregnant, the nursing mother, and the elderly.

## Practical Preparation for the Body and Home

A productive Ramadan is rarely the product of inspiration alone. It is usually the result of small logistical decisions made before the month begins.

- **Adjust sleep early.** Shift bedtime gradually so that waking for Suhoor and praying Fajr is not a struggle.
- **Reduce caffeine and sugar in the weeks before.** The first three days of withdrawal are easier outside Ramadan than inside it.
- **Plan modest meals.** Heavy Iftars steal energy from Taraweeh. Decide in advance that food will serve worship, not the other way round.
- **Set a charity plan.** Automate a daily sadaqah, however small, so that not a single day of the month passes without giving.
- **Protect the last ten nights.** If possible, arrange time off work for the final ten days and nights, when Laylat al-Qadr is most likely.

## Making Ramadan 2026 Count

The month will pass whether or not we are ready for it. What changes from one Ramadan to the next is the believer who meets it. Set two or three specific, measurable goals rather than a long list of vague intentions. A daily juz of Qur'an, Taraweeh every night, and one act of hidden charity each day is more transformative than a sprawling plan that collapses by the second week.

Above all, ask Allah now to let you reach Ramadan 1447 in good health and iman, and to accept your worship within it. Reaching the month is itself a gift not promised to anyone.
