A masjid named \"As-Sunnah\" reflects a commitment to reviving and practising Islam according to the authentic traditions of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, serving as a beacon for the community to learn, worship, and implement the Sunnah in daily life.

## The Meaning of \"Sunnah\" in the Quran

The term \"Sunnah\" appears in the Quran with profound significance, referring to both the established way of Allah and the exemplary path of His messengers. Allah says:

<Arabic>{\"سُنَّةَ مَن قَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا قَبْلَكَ مِن رُّسُلِنَا ۖ وَلَا تَجِدُ لِسُنَّتِنَا تَحْوِيلًا"}</Arabic>

*"This was Our Sunnah (rule or way) with the Messengers We sent before you (O Muhammad صلى الله عليه و سلم), and you will not find any alteration in Our Sunnah (rule or way)."* <Citation source="Quran" book="Quran" number="17:77" href="https://quran.com/17/77" />

This verse establishes that Allah's way (Sunnah) with His prophets is consistent and unchangeable. It underscores that the Sunnah is not merely a set of rituals but a divine methodology for guidance that has been preserved across all prophecies.

Similarly, Allah commands the wives of the Prophet ﷺ to remember what is recited in their homes:

<Arabic>{"وَٱذْكُرْنَ مَا يُتْلَىٰ فِى بُيُوتِكُنَّ مِنْ ءَايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلْحِكْمَةِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ لَطِيفًا خَبِيرًا"}</Arabic>

*"And remember (O you the members of the Prophet’s family, the Graces of your Lord), that which is recited in your houses of the Verses of Allâh and Al-Hikmah (i.e. Prophet’s Sunnah - legal ways)..."* <Citation source="Quran" book="Quran" number="33:34" href="https://quran.com/33/34" />

Here, "Al-Hikmah" is interpreted by early scholars as the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ, showing that the Quran and Sunnah are inseparable sources of divine guidance. The Prophet's household was a living example of how the Quran and Sunnah intertwine, and thus a masjid bearing the name "As-Sunnah" reminds believers of this holistic understanding of Islam.

Allah further warns about the consequences of abandoning the Sunnah:

<Arabic>{"فَهَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّا سُنَّتَ ٱلْأَوَّلِينَ ۚ فَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًا ۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ ٱللَّهِ تَحْوِيلًا"}</Arabic>

*"Then, can they expect anything (else) but the Sunnah (way of dealing) of the peoples of old? So no change will you find in Allâh’s Sunnah (way of dealing), and no turning off will you find in Allâh’s Sunnah (way of dealing)."* <Citation source="Quran" book="Quran" number="35:43" href="https://quran.com/35/43" />

This verse connects the Sunnah of Allah—His consistent justice and punishment for those who reject His messengers—with the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ, which is the path to avoid such punishment. A masjid dedicated to the Sunnah thus serves as a sanctuary where the community can learn this divine pattern and seek refuge from deviation.

## The Significance of "Masjid" in the Quran

The word "masjid" itself holds immense spiritual weight. The Quran mentions two sacred mosques, highlighting their role as places of worship and divine connection:

<Arabic>{"سُبْحَـٰنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ لَيْلًا مِّنَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ إِلَى ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْأَقْصَا ٱلَّذِى بَـٰرَكْنَا حَوْلَهُۥ لِنُرِيَهُۥ مِنْ ءَايَـٰتِنَآ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْبَصِيرُ"}</Arabic>

*"Glorious is He Who made his servant travel by night from Al-Masjid-ul-Harām to Al-Masjid-ul-AqSā whose environs We have blessed, so that We let him see some of Our signs. Surely, He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing."* <Citation source="Quran" book="Quran" number="17:1" href="https://quran.com/17/1" />

This verse establishes the sanctity of the masjid as a place where Allah manifests His signs and blessings. A masjid named "As-Sunnah" aspires to be a local embodiment of this concept—a blessed space where the community can witness the signs of Allah through the study and practice of the Sunnah.

## The Role of a Masjid As-Sunnah in the Community

A masjid carrying the name "As-Sunnah" is more than a building; it is a statement of purpose. It commits to reviving the Prophetic model in all aspects of worship and community life. This includes strict adherence to authentic hadith in prayer, fasting, and other acts of devotion, as well as fostering an environment where the Sunnah is taught, understood, and implemented without innovation.

The Quranic verses we have cited establish that the Sunnah is a divinely preserved path. A masjid As-Sunnah thus becomes a center for Quranic and hadith study, where believers can deepen their understanding of the Qur'an's verses alongside the Prophet's explanations. It serves as a guardian against the changes and innovations that some communities may introduce into the religion.

Moreover, the masjid should strive to embody the Prophetic character—mercy, patience, and justice—in its dealings with both Muslims and non-Muslims. It is not merely a place of ritual but a community hub that reflects the holistic nature of the Sunnah, including social responsibility, family values, and ethical business practices.

## Reviving the Sunnah Today

In an era when many communities face spiritual challenges and divisions, a masjid As-Sunnah represents a beacon of authenticity. The Quranic promise that "no change will you find in Allah's Sunnah" (35:43) gives confidence that the path of the Prophet ﷺ remains valid and effective for all times.

The name challenges Muslims to continuously learn, implement, and teach the Sunnah. It calls for a return to the original sources of Islam—the Quran and the authentic hadith—while avoiding blind imitation of cultural practices that may contradict the Prophetic model.

A masjid As-Sunnah should therefore prioritize classes on the science of hadith, weekly gatherings for the study of authentic traditions, and practical workshops on applying the Sunnah in daily life—from the etiquette of eating and sleeping to dealings in trade and governance. When the community sees the beauty and completeness of the Sunnah, it becomes a living testimony to the mercy brought by the Prophet ﷺ.
