Surah Al-Baqarah, the longest chapter in the Quran, holds immense significance, and the last two verses (ayat 285-286) are especially cherished for their profound protection and blessings, bestowing mercy on believers every night.

## The Virtue of Surah Al-Baqarah's Last Two Ayats

The final two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah are among the most celebrated in the Quran. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ emphasized their unique status. It is reported through authentic narrations that whoever recites these two verses at night, they will be sufficient for him as a protection against any harm. <Citation source="sunnah.com search" book="last 2 ayats of surah baqarah" number="N/A" href="https://sunnah.com/search?q=last%202%20ayats%20of%20surah%20baqarah" />

This hadith highlights the protective power of these verses, which encompass a declaration of faith, a plea for forgiveness, and a supplication for mercy. Reciting them serves as a spiritual shield before sleep.

## The Content and Meaning of the Last Two Ayats

The verses themselves are a beautiful encapsulation of core Islamic beliefs. They affirm faith in Allah, His angels, His books, and His messengers, making no distinction between any of the prophets. They also contain a powerful dua seeking Allah’s forgiveness and mercy.

<Arabic>
{أَمَّنِ ٱلرَّسُولُ بِمَاۤ أُنزِلَ إِلَیۡهِ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ وَٱلۡمُؤۡمِنُونَۚ كُلٌّ ءَامَنَ بِٱلـلَّهِ وَمَلَـٰۤىِٕكَتِهِۦ وَكُتُبِهِۦ وَرُسُلِهِۦ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَیۡنَ أَحَدٍ مِّن رُّسُلِهِۦۚ وَقَالُوا۟ سَمِعۡنَا وَأَطَعۡنَاۖ غُفۡرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَیۡكَ ٱلۡمَصِیرُ}
</Arabic>

*Lā yukallifu llāhu nafsan illā wus‘ahā. Lahā mā kasabat wa ‘alayhā maktasabat. Rabbanā lā tu’ākhidhnā in nasīnā aw akhta’nā. Rabbanā wa lā tahmil ‘alaynā isran kamā hamaltahū ‘ala lladhīna min qablinā. Rabbanā wa lā tuhammilnā mā lā tāqata lanā bihī. Wa‘fu ‘annā, waghfir lanā, warhamnā. Anta mawlānā fansurnā ‘ala l-qawmi l-kāfirīn.* (Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:285-286)

(Translation: The Messenger has believed in what was revealed to him from his Lord, and [so have] the believers. All of them have believed in Allah and His angels and His books and His messengers, [saying], "We make no distinction between any of His messengers." And they say, "We hear and we obey. [We seek] Your forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the [final] destination." Allah does not charge a soul except [with that within] its capacity. It will have [the consequence of] what [good] it has gained, and it will bear [the consequence of] what [evil] it has earned. "Our Lord, do not impose blame upon us if we have forgotten or erred. Our Lord, and lay not upon us a burden like that which You laid upon those before us. Our Lord, and burden us not with that which we have no ability to bear. And pardon us; and forgive us; and have mercy upon us. You are our protector, so give us victory over the disbelieving people.")

## The Blessings of Reciting and Memorizing the Last Two Ayats

The hadith regarding the last two ayats of Surah Al-Baqarah indicates that reciting them at night brings profound divine protection. The Prophet ﷺ stated that these two verses were given from the treasure under the Throne. <Citation source="sunnah.com search" book="last 2 ayats of surah baqarah" number="N/A" href="https://sunnah.com/search?q=last%202%20ayats%20of%20surah%20baqarah" />

This treasure is a special mercy from Allah. Reciting these verses is an act of worship that opens the door to blessings, forgiveness, and safeguarding from evil. Many scholars encourage memorizing them and incorporating them into the nightly routine for their immense spiritual benefits.

## Full Recitation and Guidance

For the full recitation of Surah Al-Baqarah, including its last two ayats, one may refer to a recording by a qualified reciter to learn the correct pronunciation (tajweed). The entire surah is a comprehensive guide, containing laws, stories, and lessons. The last two verses serve as a concise summary of faith and supplication.

The Quran itself describes the purpose of such clear signs. Allah says:

<Arabic>
{سُورَةٌ أَنزَلْنَـٰهَا وَفَرَضْنَـٰهَا وَأَنزَلْنَا فِيهَآ ءَايَـٰتٍۭ بَيِّنَـٰتٍ لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَذَكَّرُونَ}
</Arabic>

*Sūratun anzalnāhā wa faraḍnāhā wa anzalnā fīhā āyātin bayyinātin la‘allakum tadhakkarūn.* (Quran 24:1)

Translation: (This is) a *Sūrah* (chapter of the Qur’ān) which We have sent down and which We have enjoined, (ordained its laws); and in it We have revealed manifest *Ayāt* (proofs, evidence, verses, lessons, signs, revelations - lawful and unlawful things, and set boundaries of Islāmic Religion), that you may remember. <Citation source="https://quran.com/24/1" book="Quran 24:1" number="24:1" href="https://quran.com/24/1" />

These clear verses are a means of remembrance and guidance for those who seek it.

## Conclusion on Practice

Making it a habit to recite the last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah before sleeping is a simple yet powerful act of worship. It aligns with the teachings of the Prophet ﷺ and brings a believer closer to divine protection and mercy. May Allah grant us all the success to act upon this noble sunnah.
